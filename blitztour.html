Why BlitzTour?

A group project during the UX/UI program.
Three people, then two — one left early. The brief: a tour app for people with limited time in a place they may not have chosen. Layovers, transfers, the waiting hours in between. Time that exists but is rarely used well.

Research to high-fidelity prototype. The scope stayed tight because the constraint was real — restricted time is not a feature request; it is the entire product logic. Every screen had to justify itself against that constraint.

Project

Efficient Touring.

Community-driven spots, cultural picks, trending places.
Matched to the time you have.

Work split organically. I took layout, components, hierarchy, and structure. My partner took visual assets, imagery, and content. No formal role assignment — each gravitated toward what they understood and the split held without negotiation.
Where preferences differed — mostly color and visual direction — I either merged both positions or adapted the structure to make my partner's preference work within the layout. The discipline was not agreement. It was integration without friction.
I drove two decisions that shaped the outcome: the layout and information structure early, and the move to high fidelity before the concept was fully resolved. Working at high fidelity forced decisions that wireframes would have deferred.
